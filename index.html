Russia: Leading with 21st Century Skills
Partnerships for Transformative Action

The World Bank is working to support the development of a partnership of state and non-state actors for the provision of 21st century skills in the Russian Federation. We aim to transform ideas and rhetoric into practical educational interventions, policy measures and sustainable institutional arrangements.

Why This Project - Now?

The education sector around the world is on the cusp of disruption from within as well as from without. The days are fast approaching when the classrooms with rows of desks and the teacher/lecturer in front will be considered as historical relics. Change has been slow to come, but there is reason to believe that the pace of change has begun to accelerate. The traditional educational model will inevitably be replaced by an emerging model with a focus on 21st century skills – creativity, collaboration, critical thinking, adaptability and so on. Ubiquitous computing devices, artificial intelligence and robotics are set to fundamentally alter the world of work, leisure and education. Countries which can master the looming educational transformation are set to be competitive and secure for their citizens the shared prosperity and peacefulness desired by all. Leadership of the Russian Federation means vastly improved potential to help not only its own citizens, but also to support development in countries around the globe.

LearnAbler:
Teacher virtual platform for 21st Century Skills

A teacher virtual platform is a digital application, available on web browsers as well as through a smart phone app that allows teachers to interact with each other and with pedagogical and technical experts. LearnAbler will be a virtual platform for the teachers in Russia to help them improve their students' 21st century skills.

We invite pedagogical experts, software developers, business people, foundations, teachers, schools… to join us in developing LearnAbler!

LearnCoding:
What’s up with Russian Scratchers?

A scratcher is a person, usually a child of age 8 to 16, who is learning important strategies for solving problems, designing projects, and communicating ideas by using a specially designed computer programming language and collaboration platform called ‘Scratch'. A puzzling disconnect that we are addressing is that only 8,351 of the 21 million scratchers are from Russia. LearnCoding is an action oriented project to at least double the number of scratchers in Russia.

We invite volunteers who can help us implement LearnCoding to join us at our Workshop in Moscow!

LearnBadge:
Sociometers for Socioemotional Skills

Sociometers are badges equipped with micro electro-mechanical gyros, accelerometers, and sensors to capture intra- and inter-personal data. They provide a breakthrough ‘bio-metric monitoring’ opportunity for measurement and development of socioemotional skills. LearnBadge will be a reliable method for measuring socioemotional data that can be useful for children and teachers.

We invite universities, technology leaders, schools and businesses to join us at our workshop in Moscow to develop LearnBadge together!
